Facial hair grows in 5 different areas: 

The mustache - the hair above the upper lip. 
The soul patch, flavor savor, or jazz dot - the strip of hair directly underneath the bottom lip. 
The goatee - the hair on the front of the chin, above the jawline, and expanding to the cheeks. 
The side-burns or mutton-chops - the hair on the cheeks above the jawline. 
The neck - everything that grows below the jawline.

chin strip (a vertical line of hair down center of the chin)

ANCHOR BEARD - A pointed beard that traces the jawline, paired with a musta

pencil mustache - thin short devlish just above the top lip
parted pencil mustache - thin short dastardly, indent above the lip is shaved bare
lampshade mustache - smaller than top lip, entirely above top lip, neat
chevron mustache - covers entire top lip, wider than lip, curves slightly down.
painters brush - like chevron but straight line, natural stache
walrus mustache - big bold thick covers entire top lip and much of the bottom one.
horseshoe mustache - covers upper lip with long bars pointing downward, includes
    cheek hair, chine shaved in the middle.
original stache -- trim mustache that sits above the top lip
handlebar - thick, long mustache with ends curled upwards and waxed.
english -- thinner mustache with ends waxed straight
hungarian -- long walrus with upward swooping handles (wyatt earp)
Dali -- pencil mustache with long ends waxed upwards.
fu manchu -- mustache grown from top corners of the mouth only, center shaved,
    groomed to long, trailing ends
