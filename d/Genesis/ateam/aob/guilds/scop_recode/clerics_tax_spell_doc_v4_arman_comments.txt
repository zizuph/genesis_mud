==================================================
ELEMENTAL CLERICS SPELLS DESCRIPTION AND BREAKDOWN
==================================================

Some of the spells below require ritual components to be made. 
These are created in the ritual chamber of the temple and require
two ingredients each to make them. The first ingredient is either
a herb or leftover. The second ingredient is a gem shard (these
can be created by crushing gems in the equipment room (this
generates 2 + random(2) shards from each gem crushed) or Earth
branch clerics can smash gems into shard using a spell (this
generates whatever the base spell generates for each gem
shattered - currently 2 +random(6) shards).

Once placed in the ritual chamber, the player will perform
the appropriate ritual for the spell (based on the spell name)
or the Diety name) and it will generate 2 + random(2) ritual 
components.

For tax calculations, I have used the mid point (due to the 
randomness of random) of the best case scenario (Earth cleric
shattering the gems for the highest yield possible per gem).
I will take the total cost of the first ingredient (herb or
leftover) and divide that by 3 (mid-point of that possible 
outcome). I will take the total cost of the second ingredient
(gem) and divide that by 5 (mid-point of that possible outcome).
I will then add both of those costs together and divide that
by 3 (mid-point of that possible outcome) to come up with an
average cost of the component per casting.

For spells that require components, I will list the ingredients
needed and the average cost per component in the list below.

================================================================
                         =============
                         Master Spells
                         =============
================================================================

Upgraded Child spells will require no components and will be
taxed at the 1.5% rate accordingly with one exception, Aleipso.
Aleipso at the Master rank and higher will cost a component
for the Fire, Air and Earth branches - Water and Spirit will
remain component free.

Master spells will start to require components if the spell 
doesn't fit your 'branch' style (i.e. damage for fire, healing
for water, etc...). If the spell does fit your branch (i.e.
a damage spell for the fire branch), it will not require a 
component and will be taxed at the 1.5% rate accordingly. If
the spell does not fit your branch (i.e. a healing spell for
the fire branch), it will require components and will be 
taxed accordingly.

The exception to this is the at-will spells, these will be
component free (they are really the only 'combat special'
that every branch gets) and will be taxed at the 1.5% rate.

Special notes for Syndyasmos spells:
====================================
The code for casting these is in the lay_cleric_soul under the esyndyasmos routine. 
It checks to make sure that the caster has a partner set, that the partner is in the 
same room as the caster, that they are teamed with the partner, that both the caster 
and the partner have enough mana to cast the spell, that both the caster and the 
partner have enough Chymos (conduit energy) stored to cast the spell, that both the 
caster and the partner aren't already concentrating on something else (including 
spells being cast), that the caster hasn't already cast the spell and they aren't 
in Syndyasmos cooldown (30 seconds), and that the caster is already in combat. If 
all of those checks pass, it then removes the Chymos (conduit energy) from both the 
caster and the partner, it removes the mana from the partner, sets the flag that 
the caster has cast the spell (starts the cooldown) and then finally casts the
appropriate spell.
